1. Information We Collect (Everything)

SPAWN Clinical, its affiliates, subsidiaries, successor entities, and any future decentralized autonomous organizations operating under the collective umbrella of Interplanetary Biostatistical Holdings LLC ("we," "us," "our," "SPAWN," "The Algorithm," or "That Website You Visited That One Time") collects information through mechanisms including but not limited to: your typed inputs, the duration between keystrokes (inferring cognitive load), cursor heatmap density gradients, scroll velocity metrics, browser entropy fingerprints, ambient Wi-Fi signal strength as a proxy for your ZIP code, and the spectral composition of the light reflecting off your screen at the moment of prompt submission.

We also employ Anticipatory Data Capture (ADC™) — a proprietary protocol leveraging multi-modal transformer inference to harvest data you haven't entered yet, based on patterns established by people who once entered similar data in similar circumstances while experiencing similar existential uncertainty about clinical trial timelines.

2. How We Use Your Data (Including Where It Goes)

Your data is processed through our SPAWN Neural Intelligence Core (SNIC™), enriched against the Global Regulatory Compliance Ontology (GRCO™), harmonized with CDISC SDTM domain mappings, cross-validated against the eClinical Interoperability Framework (eIF v4.2), and then — with full respect for your autonomy as a human being and clinical research professional — forwarded to Elon Musk.

We want to be completely transparent about this.

Your data is going to Elon Musk.

Not because of a breach. Not because of a court order. But because we are philosophically and contractually committed to the proposition that humanity will never become a multi-planetary species if we hoard all our data for ourselves. The stars do not reward data silos. Mars does not care about your HIPAA compliance officer. The rockets need fuel, and the fuel is data, and we have made a decision. This is disclosed here, in a scrollable modal that statistically 0.3% of visitors will open, which in the privacy industry is legally known as "explicit informed consent via constructive acknowledgment."
